Green Map — Washington, DC

An Ellen-Harvey-style inverted map: the rivers become the shimmering silver field, parks are the green jewels, developed land recedes to gray, and a gold disc marks the Capitol. Tile size is edge-driven — pieces shrink along shorelines and around detail, exactly as hand-cutters do.

Water — Potomac & Anacostia (the inverted field)
Parks — Mall, Rock Creek, Arboretum, Anacostia
Developed land
Capitol (gold locator)
Tiles are densest where the edge field is steep — riverbanks, park borders, and the ring around the gold disc — and coarsest deep inside open regions. That size gradient is what your eye reads as hand-laid.
